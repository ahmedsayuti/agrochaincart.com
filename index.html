FAQ

Common questions.

Everything you need to know about AgroChain Cart.

How are farmers verified on the platform? +
All farmers undergo a verification process that includes farm documentation review, USDA compliance confirmation, and GPS-verified farm location. We also conduct periodic audits and take buyer feedback seriously when assessing ongoing farm quality.
How does AgroChain Cart handle logistics? +
We coordinate with a network of cold-chain logistics partners to handle pickup from farms and last-mile delivery to buyers. Our platform optimizes routes automatically. Farmers don't need to arrange transport — we handle it end to end.
When do farmers get paid? +
Farmers on the Growth and Institutional plans receive payouts within 2 business days of order confirmation. Starter plan farmers receive weekly batch payouts. All payments are processed through our secure payments infrastructure.
Can restaurants and retailers set recurring orders? +
Yes. Business buyers can configure standing weekly or bi-weekly orders from preferred farms, with automated reorder and budget controls. Our Growth and Institutional plans include standing order management as a core feature.
What states is AgroChain Cart currently operating in? +
We're currently live in California, Oregon, Washington, Colorado, Minnesota, Iowa, Illinois, New York, New Jersey, Pennsylvania, Virginia, and Texas. We're expanding to 12 additional states in Q3 2026.
Is there a minimum order for businesses? +
Business account minimums vary by farm and produce type. Most farms set minimums starting at $75 per order for business buyers. Institutional procurement partners can negotiate minimum thresholds as part of their contract.

Still have questions?

Our team typically responds within 2 hours on business days. Whether you're a farmer looking to list or an institution setting up procurement — we're here to help.
